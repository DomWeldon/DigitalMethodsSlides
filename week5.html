Doing a Digital Methods Research Project

1. Introduction

What are you researching, and why is it important? Does the topic link to any relevant literature? Wider issues...

Narrow down what your topic to a set of Research Questions (RQs).

Number your RQs in a list and refer back to them throughout.

2. Methodology

What method(s) have you chosen, and why? How does it relate to your RQs? (integrated presentation)

What did you do? Document and show your working!

"Using XXX methos as described by YYY et al. (20XX), I did ABC"

3. Results / Findings

What did you observe as the results of your DM project?

Factual: figures, charts, tables and so on.

4. Discussion and Evaluation

How can you relate your results to your RQs?

How effective was your methodology at addressing your RQs?

What would you do differently?

Relate to wider issues - DISCUSS!

Group Exercise: Your Turn on the Whiteboards

In groups of three, I want you to undertake a practice digital methods research project and produce a plan on a sheet of flipchart paper, just like I did last week

You can choose any topic to research, but I want you to produce the four different sections (in quadrants) like I did on the whiteboard, and show how you would take a research method, devse research questions, and then produce a report plan based on either preliminary or supposed findings.

Each team will report back to the rest of the group in the final few minutes of the seminar.
